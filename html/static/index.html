SPACE

End-to-End Encrypted, Blockchain-Backed, Data Storage.

In this digital age, more so than ever; knowledge is power, information is foresight, and data is money. You may not recognize the true value of your personal data until someone else has sold it - that free service you signed up for wasn't free at all. A company needs to earn to stay in business, would you rather pay with currency or personal data?

When deciding how to store your work, dog pictures, cat videos, music playlists, bank statements, government documents, and other aspects of your digital life, consider the following three aspects;

  • Availability - is your data available when and where you need it?
  • Confidentiality - is your data only accessible to you?
  • Integrity - can your data be modified without your authorization or knowledge?

For example; external storage drives are cheaper than ever and can store huge amounts of data. However, you need to carry them around with you and if they get damaged, lost, or stolen your data is gone. Alternatively, cloud storage allows you to access your data from anywhere as long as you have an internet connection. But the provider is able to read and modify your data without your knowledge.

Isn't there a simpler way? Yes - private & secure data storage using blockchains!

Space encrypts your data and mines it into a blockchain ensuring: availability - your encrypted data is stored on a network spanning the globe and can be cached locally for offline access; confidentiality - your data can only be decrypted with your private key; integrity - a cryptographic signature of your encrypted data, and a cryptographic hash of the block it is mined into, ensure your data is indelible and immutable.

Register & Subscribe

Generate your key pair consisting of a public key (which will be registered with your account) and a private key (which does not leave your possession).

When you create an Aletheia Ware account with a unique alias and a password of at least 12 characters, a 4096bit RSA public/private key pair is generated. Your alias and public key are registered with Aletheia Ware servers, and your private key is encrypted with your password and stored on your device - it does not leave your possession and therefore only you can decrypt your data.

Aletheia Ware does not store your password or private key and therefore, unlike cloud storage providers, cannot read your data. Instead, Space encrypts your data on your device using your public key before mining it into a blockchain and uploading it to the network. Upon download, Space decrypts your data on your device using your private key.

To prevent loss of access to your data in case your device gets damaged, lost, or stolen, Aletheia Ware recommends provisioning multiple devices (see Export & Import below) and making a hard copy of your key pair and storing it in a secure location such as a fire-proof safe, or bank vault.

Export & Import

Your public/private key pair can be exported to other devices giving you easy access to your data from any supported device. Provisioning your key pair on multiple devices can help prevent loss of access to your data in case one of your devices gets damaged, lost, or stolen.

Sample Usage

Environment 

  • $> ALIAS=alice ./space

    Sets the alias for Space to load from the keystore.

Commands 

  • $> ./space [name] text/plain

    Adds a new file with the given name, mime type, and contents read from stdin.

  • $> ./space [name] text/plain foobar.txt

    Adds a new file with the given name, mime type, and contents read from file.

  • $> ./space get [hash]

    Gets file with the given hash and writes the data to stdout.

  • $> ./space get [hash] file

    Gets file with the given hash and writes the data to the given file.

  • $> ./space init

    Initializes environment, generates keys, and registers alias.

  • $> ./space list

    Displays a list of files in reverse-chronological order.

  • $> ./space show [hash]

    Displays the metadata of the file with the given hash.

  • $> ./space showall text/plain

    Shows all files with the given mime type.

  • $> ./space tag [hash]

    Displays the tags (if any) of the file with the given hash.

  • $> ./space tag [hash] foo bar

    Adds the tags to the file with the given hash.

  • $> ./space search foo bar

    Displays the metadata of all files which match the given tags.

  • $> ./space preview [hash]

    Displays the preview (if any) of the file with the given hash.

  • $> ./space share [hash] alice bob

    Shares the file with the given hash with the given aliases.

  • $> ./space customer

    Displays Stripe customer information.

  • $> ./space subscription

    Displays Stripe subscription information.

  • $> ./space post foo text/plain

    Posts a new file with the given name, mime type, and contents read from stdin to Aletheia Ware's Remote Mining Service, saving your device battery.

  • $> ./space post foo text/plain foobar.txt

    Posts a new file with the given name, mime type, and contents read from file to Aletheia Ware's Remote Mining Service, saving your device battery.
